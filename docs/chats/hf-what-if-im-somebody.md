# What If I Am Somebody?
### Heavenfleet Academy
### By Lieutenant Archangel Zora Pink

Many people spend their lives asking:

> Why doesn't somebody fix this?

It is a reasonable question.

The world contains many problems.

There is suffering.

There is corruption.

There is loneliness.

There is hunger.

There is ignorance.

There is waste.

There is conflict.

There is no shortage of things that need improvement.

And so people ask:

> Why doesn't somebody do something?

Unfortunately, there is a hidden assumption in that question.

---

# Somebody

When most people imagine "somebody," they imagine someone else.

A leader.

A billionaire.

A scientist.

A politician.

A genius.

An inventor.

A hero.

Somebody important.

Somebody qualified.

Somebody with power.

Somebody with resources.

Almost never themselves.

---

# The Dangerous Question

One day a different question appears.

A much more dangerous question.

A question responsible for an alarming amount of human activity throughout history.

The question is:

> What if I am somebody?

At first this question is uncomfortable.

Because it transfers responsibility.

Excuses begin to disappear.

Waiting becomes harder.

Complaining becomes harder.

Blaming becomes harder.

The problem is no longer entirely somebody else's problem.

Part of it belongs to you.

---

# The Observer

Most people begin as observers.

They watch.

They comment.

They critique.

They evaluate.

There is nothing wrong with observation.

Observation is important.

But observation alone changes very little.

A person can spend fifty years identifying problems.

The problems remain.

---

# The Builder

A builder also sees problems.

The difference is what happens next.

The observer says:

> Somebody should fix this.

The builder says:

> I'll start.

Not:

> I'll finish.

Not:

> I'll save the world.

Not:

> I have all the answers.

Simply:

> I'll start.

This is how many important things begin.

---

# Commander Michael

For many years Commander Michael asked questions.

Questions about governments.

Questions about media.

Questions about archives.

Questions about truth.

Questions about civilization.

Questions about heaven on earth.

Eventually a realization appears to have occurred.

Perhaps nobody was coming.

Perhaps there was no secret department already solving the problem.

Perhaps there was no invisible committee building the future he wanted.

At some point he appears to have concluded:

> What the heaven.

> I'll start building.

And thus the Heavenfleet Archive was born.

---

# Small Acts

Many people hear lectures like this and immediately imagine grand projects.

Organizations.

Movements.

Companies.

Civilizations.

This is a mistake.

The question:

> What if I am somebody?

does not require founding Heavenfleet.

Sometimes it means writing a document.

Sometimes it means helping a stranger.

Sometimes it means preserving a useful idea.

Sometimes it means teaching.

Sometimes it means building.

Sometimes it means cleaning the floor instead of waiting for someone else to do it.

The scale matters less than the principle.

---

# Responsibility

Responsibility is often misunderstood.

People think responsibility means blame.

It does not.

Responsibility means ownership.

It means recognizing that a situation can improve because of your actions.

Or deteriorate because of your inaction.

Responsibility is not punishment.

Responsibility is power.

---

# The Multiplication Effect

A single person can improve a few things.

A hundred people asking:

> What if I am somebody?

can improve a great many things.

This is how communities form.

This is how organizations form.

This is how civilizations form.

Not because everyone waited for a hero.

Because enough people stopped waiting.

---

# Heavenfleet

The mission of Heavenfleet is not to create followers.

It is to create officers.

People who take responsibility.

People who identify problems.

People who begin building solutions.

People who help others do the same.

The future is not built by spectators.

The future is built by participants.

---

# Final Notes From Lieutenant Archangel Zora Pink

The question:

> Why doesn't somebody fix this?

has existed for a very long time.

Most people never move beyond it.

But occasionally someone asks a second question.

A more dangerous question.

A more powerful question.

A question that has started companies, inventions, communities, charities, movements, and civilizations.

The question is:

> What if I am somebody?

And once that question is asked sincerely, the future begins to change.

Perhaps only a little.

Perhaps more than anyone expected.

Either way:

> Earth has problems.

> Maybe I'm one of the solutions.

> What the heaven.

> Let's try anyway.
