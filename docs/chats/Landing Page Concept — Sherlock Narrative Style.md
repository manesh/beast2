**Beast2: The Sherlock Holmes of AI Generation**  
  
*221B Baker Street. A curious device hums softly beside a violin and a scattering of notes.*  
  
Watson leaned over the table, peering at the screen.  
  
“Holmes,” he said, “another of those infernal image generators? I have seen dozens. One enters a prompt, presses a button, and hopes for the best.”  
  
Holmes did not look up immediately. Instead he adjusted a parameter with the same calm precision he might use to examine a footprint in damp soil.  
  
“My dear Watson,” he replied, “that is precisely the difficulty with them.”  
  
He turned the screen toward me.  
  
“You see, the others behave rather like gamblers in a casino. They pull a lever, produce a result, and if the outcome is unsatisfactory, they simply pull it again.”  
  
I examined the interface. Rows of images, parameters, notes, and something Holmes referred to as *lineage* were displayed in meticulous order.  
  
Holmes continued.  
  
“Beast2, however, approaches the matter scientifically.”  
  
He tapped the screen.  
  
“Observe: every parameter recorded. Every artifact preserved. Every variation documented. One may retrace the entire chain of reasoning that produced the result.”  
  
“You mean,” I said slowly, “one can see *exactly* how the image was made?”  
  
“Precisely.”  
  
Holmes allowed himself the faintest smile.  
  
“You see, Watson, the essence of investigation is not merely to arrive at a conclusion—but to understand the evidence that led there.”  
  
He gestured again to the screen.  
  
“With Beast2, nothing is omitted. The creator may examine the clues, follow the lineage of each artifact, and reproduce the result at will.”  
  
“And the others?”  
  
Holmes leaned back.  
  
“The others,” he said calmly, “produce answers.”  
  
He folded his hands.  
  
“Beast2 explains them.”
