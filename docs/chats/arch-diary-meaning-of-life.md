over the last few years ive watched perhaps a hundred or more testimonies of ndes, obes, astral projection, remote viewing, and channelers.

human consciousness seems to have access to something that seems like an ai system. by intent or verbal command, the universal system seems to be activated.

my own experience says i am connected to some kind of system beyond my 5 senses.

this significantly changed my worldview. i used to be an atheist, believing we  only had one life to live, that memories were stored in the brain and consciousness was a result of electrochemical processes. but given my own experience and the testimony of many others, my beliefs have changed.

i spent many years after my own first "spiritual experiences" wandering. adrift, confused, constantly asking "why? what do you want?" to god, or whoever or whatever is listening and responsing.

i still ask these questions but an answer? no clear and unambiguous answer seems to be forthcoming.

on earth, the physical answers might seem simple enough. "survive. reproduce. hoard." yet if everyone goes for these objectives, we get an ugly world. and as ugly as some things are, people are not that ugly, though some may seem to be at times.

i used to fear death. i was motivated by fear of death to learn, to work. i was extremely cautious.

but since my experiences of someone saying to me, "i am your higher guidance" through an invisible medium, ive had to contend with undeniable subjective evidence that there is someone watching me, and not just watching passively, actively communicating and revealing themselves.

i dont know if this experience has made me a "better person." but one thing that has changed is testimonies i disregarded as hallucination i now listen to, memorize, and try to understand.

im still a skeptic. i seem to have been since an early age, when i asked "why are we memorizing history books which could be lies instead of learning useful skills and repeatable scientific experiments and logic operations?"

but maybe im a hopeful skeptic now.

reminds me. there are "laws of logic."

i think i need to write a lecture "the laws of the skeptic." many of my axioms could be considered "laws of the skeptic" already.
