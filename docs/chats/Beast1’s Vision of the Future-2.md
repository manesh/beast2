**Beast1’s Vision of the Future**  
  
It’s been ten years since I released Beast2 and Beast3.  
  
I query my handheld.  
  
“Minibeast, status of gens?”  
  
The reply is immediate.  
  
“66% complete. Estimated time remaining: two hours.”  
  
Good.  
  
I feel my stomach growling.  
  
Beast1 needs food.  
  
“Minibeast, the usual meal.”  
  
“Estimated delivery in five minutes.”  
  
Five minutes.  
  
Yeah, I chose to live far from the nearest auto-restaurant powered by Beast3’s **prepare_food** creation. The food itself generally takes seconds to prepare, and the drones are fast and quiet — not annoying buzzards — but still… five minutes?  
  
I’m really out here in the woods.  
  
And honestly, I like it that way.  
  
⸻  
  
**Quiet Infrastructure**  
  
All farms are Beast3 powered now.  
  
Farmers and engineers mostly just check status indicators and fix bugs, but they’re few and far between.  
  
The creations that serve human needs — food, shelter, clothing — are some of the most mature pipelines in the archive. They’ve been refined through years of collaboration, documentation, and constant iteration.  
  
People improve them the way open source software used to be improved — except now the creations themselves are the infrastructure of civilization.  
  
⸻  
  
**How It Happened**  
  
Once I put Beast2 in people’s hands, the rest just… kind of happened.  
  
People created.  
  
They shared archives.  
  
They improved pipelines.  
  
They ran experiments.  
  
Creations evolved faster than any single person or company could manage.  
  
Civilization started running on collaborative creation systems.  
  
⸻  
  
**Today**  
  
Come to think of it, I should probably check the feeds.  
  
There are so many games and movies being made now that it’s hard to keep up.  
  
Entire universes are being generated and refined every day.  
  
I guess that’s mostly what my gens have been running.  
  
I thought that was obvious.  
  
I open the feed to see what new games — new universes to explore — have been pushed to **beast2.ai** today.  
  
⸻  
  
**One Possible Future**  
  
At least… that’s one possible future.  
  
I can imagine others.  
  
But why waste limited compute?  
  
⸻  
  
**The Direction**  
  
I’m aiming at maximizing my joy.  
  
And that means some kind of Starfleet future — something close to the hopeful civilization imagined by Gene Roddenberry.  
  
Even if, like him, I don’t live to see it.  
  
He imagined a unified world exploring the stars.  
  
Some people even called him **“The Great Bird of the Galaxy.”**  
  
That vision stuck with me.  
  
⸻  
  
**Closing**  
  
Maybe it happens.  
  
Maybe it doesn’t.  
  
But what the Heaven… might as well give it a shot.
