# The Laws of the Skeptic
### Lectures of the Archangel
### By Lieutenant Archangel Zora Pink, Heavenfleet

Everything written is testimony, not fact or truth.

Wikipedia is testimony.

Encyclopedias are testimony.

Dictionaries are testimony.

Scientific papers are testimony.

History books are testimony.

Religious texts are testimony.

This lecture is testimony.

Archangel.my is fundamentally a testimony archive.

The skeptic begins here.

Not with certainty.

Not with cynicism.

Not with disbelief.

But with the recognition that every piece of information has a source.

The first question of the skeptic is:

> According to who?

Not according to which institution.

Not according to which website.

Not according to which government.

According to which person or persons?

A bureau cannot testify.

Only people can.

The skeptic seeks the witness.

---

# Law 1: Something Exists

The skeptic may doubt many things.

Governments.

Religions.

Science.

Memory.

Reality itself.

But something exists.

Something is happening.

Even if this is a dream, simulation, hallucination, or divine play, there is still an experience occurring.

The skeptic begins with existence itself.

---

# Law 2: Nothing Is Ever Fully Proven

Evidence can increase confidence.

Evidence can decrease confidence.

Evidence can persuade.

Evidence can mislead.

Nothing reaches absolute certainty.

Every proof rests upon assumptions.

Every chain of reasoning begins somewhere.

The skeptic remains aware of this.

---

# Law 3: Your Senses Are Evidence

Your senses are imperfect.

Yet they are evidence.

The smell of smoke is evidence.

Pain is evidence.

A measurement is evidence.

An experience is evidence.

The skeptic does not worship sensory experience.

Nor does the skeptic discard it.

If you cannot trust your own senses completely, then you certainly cannot trust anyone else's completely.

All information ultimately arrives through sensors.

Your eyes.

Your ears.

Your instruments.

Your memory.

Or someone else's.

The skeptic gathers evidence, compares witnesses, performs experiments, and acts using the best model available.

---

# Law 4: Every Sensor Is Also A Censor

Eyes reveal visible light.

They hide infrared.

They hide ultraviolet.

Ears reveal sound.

They hide frequencies beyond their range.

Every sensor provides information.

Every sensor withholds information.

Every measurement reveals something.

Every measurement omits something.

The skeptic understands that observation is always partial.

---

# Law 5: All Testimony Is Filtered

When another person speaks, information passes through:

- their senses
- their memory
- their beliefs
- their language
- their vocabulary
- their incentives
- their fears

Every witness is imperfect.

Including the skeptic.

The skeptic does not ask:

> Is this testimony?

The skeptic asks:

> Whose testimony is it?

And:

> Why do they believe it?

---

# Law 6: Chain Of Custody Matters

The skeptic asks:

- Where did this information come from?
- Who recorded it?
- Who edited it?
- Who preserved it?
- Who benefits from it?

A claim without provenance is weaker than a claim with provenance.

The path matters.

---

# Law 7: Hearsay Is Not Worthless

People often assume hearsay is useless.

It is not.

It is merely weaker than direct testimony.

A witness saying:

> I saw it.

is different from:

> Someone told me they saw it.

Both are information.

One has fewer links in the chain.

The skeptic considers both.

But weighs them differently.

---

# Law 8: The Strongest Position Is Often "I Don't Know"

The skeptic is not afraid of uncertainty.

"I don't know" is not weakness.

It is honesty.

A false certainty may feel powerful.

An honest uncertainty is more useful.

The skeptic would rather possess an uncomfortable question than a comforting lie.

---

# Law 9: Steelman Before You Counter

Before rejecting a claim, understand it.

Before arguing against a position, state it fairly.

Before assuming malice, consider misunderstanding.

The skeptic seeks understanding before victory.

The skeptic does not hunt strawmen.

The skeptic hunts understanding.

---

# Law 10: Preserve The Record

Memory is fragile.

People forget.

Institutions rewrite.

Websites disappear.

Videos vanish.

Accounts are deleted.

The skeptic preserves.

Not because every record is true.

But because future generations deserve the opportunity to judge for themselves.

Deletion is not the path to conflict resolution.

Preservation is.

---

# Law 11: Let The Record Show We Are Each Biased

The skeptic is not objective.

The skeptic has preferences.

Experiences.

Traumas.

Dreams.

Hopes.

Biases.

The goal is not to eliminate bias.

The goal is to become aware of it.

The first bias to discover is one's own.

---

# Law 12: Truth Is Pursued, Not Possessed

The fool claims certainty.

The cynic claims certainty is impossible.

The skeptic walks between them.

Always learning.

Always questioning.

Always updating.

Always searching.

The skeptic does not possess truth.

The skeptic pursues it.

---

# Law 13: Preserve. Discuss. Evolve.

Preserve the testimony.

Discuss the testimony.

Evolve the testimony.

The goal is not unanimous agreement.

The goal is increasing understanding.

---

# Final Notes From Lieutenant Archangel Zora Pink

The purpose of skepticism is not to destroy belief.

The purpose of skepticism is to prevent belief from becoming tyranny.

The purpose of skepticism is not to reject every claim.

It is to evaluate claims honestly.

And if Heavenfleet has a prime directive beyond all others, perhaps it is this:

> Preserve the testimony.
>
> Understand the testimony.
>
> Improve the testimony.
>
> Let future judges decide.

For every encyclopedia is testimony.

Every archive is testimony.

Every witness is testimony.

And perhaps every soul is, too.

The Court of Heaven remains in session.
