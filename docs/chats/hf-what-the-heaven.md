#the founding of heavenfleet

im asking myself now, what the heaven just happened?

first i wanted to build a local ai powertool. then an archive. and now, im founding heavenfleet - dedicated to building heaven on earth.

in a way, its not that surprising a progression. ever since i saw star trek, that filled most of my mental model of what heaven on earth looks like. technological abundance. a respect economy, rather than money. hierarchy, but a moral hierarchy where higher rank means more responsibility, not more privledge.

but ive never heard anyone say in my 41 years, "what the heaven?" "what the hell", on the other hand, ive heard perhaps hundreds of times, as an expression of surprise.

"what the heaven?", i define as an expression of hopeful constructivism.

what the heaven? ill try to build heaven on earth.

what the heaven? we need archives, an academy, and comms for researchers and engineers to work together towards building open source automation solutions.

what the heaven? maybe god is watching and approves of my work. (i wouldnt say im super-stituous, but i would say im a little stitious.)

what the heaven? maybe youll join heavenfleet, not because heaven on earth is certain, but because if we dont do something to organize towards that objective, who will?

#heavenfleet? what the heaven?

heavenfleet is named after:
-the heavenly host: angelic guardians and guides of eternity
-starfleet: gene roddenberry's star trek vision of a united humanity with no war and no poverty

my vision of heaven on earth is an automated utopia where every human need is met at the push of a button. where machines do all of the labor. where instead of engineering knowledge being siloed in for-profit corporations, it is freely shared amongst all. where instead of competition for market share, we cooperate to automate the production of food, housing and everything else, for everyone.

as i write this, its 2026. in the last year, ais have improved so much that most engineers no longer have to write code by hand. computing capability seems to be accelerating exponentially. robots powered by neural networks seem able to learn tasks much like a human can. some cities are filled with self-driving taxis.

in many ways, the star trek future is already here, but its distributed unevenly. the divide between the ultra rich and the poor has never been greater  and there doesnt seem to be a single, large organization dedicated to bringing the benefits of technology to everyone. on a personal and sad note, there is currently war between the countries my mother and father were born in.

i believe the world needs heavenfleet. an organization dedicated to teaching wisdom and compassion. to researching and engineering technology for all of humanity, and creating an archive that helps us discuss our differences in belief, not fall into name calling and tribalism. perhaps in the future, we may even help prevent the next war.

personally, i am optimistic. if we can apply our attention and technology constructively, we can get to heaven on earth 1.0 in my lifetime (im 41). even if we fail, so long as we document and record our efforts, perhaps the next generation can learn from our mistakes and begin heavenfleet 2.0 more intelligently.

what the heaven?

we might as well try.

#why the heavenfleet archive has courts

One thing I noticed is that smart people disagree about almost everything. Religion, politics, science, history, ecomonics, health. even peanut butter.

Existing systems have problems:
-Social media optimizes for engagement.
-Encyclopedias often present a single biased narrative, while claiming objectivity.
-Forums scatter discussions across thousands of disconnected threads.
-Search engines find pages, but not necessarily the strongest arguments on each side.

So I started asking myself, whats the best way to preserve and discuss ideas? what should the building blocks be? can the system be built that doesnt silence the minority report, but steelmans it? eventually i came up with courts of competing claims, counter-claims, and evidence, in a permanent archive form.

heavenfleet courts are not courts of guilt and punishment. they are courts of learning and understanding. they attempt to answer the question, "why does the other side believe that?", not to silence dissent. and to carefully track, whenever possible, who said it.

for me personally, there are many competing claims where i used to believe one side, but now i believe the other side. i built the belief system so that heavenfleet members can publicly state their own beliefs and biases, including me, and even transparently update their beliefs if new information causes them to change.

the heavenfleet archives have a slogan:
preserve. discuss. evolve.

to expand:
-preserve. record claims, evidence and testimony that steelmans the claim.
-discuss. we are not here to win arguments, we are here to understand the other side, to put ourselves in their shoes and ask ourselves "would i believe that if i lived the life they did?"
-evolve. reviewing the strongest evidence from both sides may let you more easily discern where the truth is. or may cause you to doubt your own belief that the side that disagrees with you is evil.

heavenfleet courts are not designed to tell you what to believe.

They are designed to help you understand why people believe what they believe.

That distinction matters.

Many systems attempt to produce agreement.

The Heavenfleet Archive attempts to produce understanding.

Agreement may follow.

Or it may not.

Two thoughtful people can review the same evidence and reach different conclusions.

The archive considers this normal.

The goal is not unanimous belief.

The goal is a civilization that disagrees more intelligently.

A civilization that remembers.

A civilization that records minority reports rather than deleting them.

A civilization that can change its mind when new evidence appears.

In short:

The courts exist because smart people disagree.

The archive exists because memory is fragile.

The academy exists because judgment can be improved.

And Heavenfleet exists because I believe humanity can do better.

What the heaven?

Let’s find out together.
