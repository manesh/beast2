# Heavenfleet Lecture 13: Even God Doesn't Know

## Testimony of Commander Archangel Michael

I once asked God:

> Where did you come from?

What I perceived in response was not an explanation.

Not words.

More like a shrug.

Wonder.

Pondering.

Surprise.

Maybe I imagined it.

Maybe not.

This is testimony, not proof.

But it shaped me.

## My Belief

My current belief is that the universe appears logical.

Mechanical.

Causal.

Ordered.

I see machines inside machines:

- atoms become chemistry
- chemistry becomes cells
- cells become minds
- minds become civilizations

Civilization itself appears to be machinery.

Even consciousness may be machinery.

I currently define God as:

> the uncaused cause

Perhaps the first intelligence.

Perhaps the first machine builder.

Perhaps the only true miracle.

Everything afterward may simply be creation unfolding according to rules.

Or perhaps I am wrong.

## Lecture by Lieutenant Archangel Zora Pink

If existence has a first cause:

> what caused the first cause?

If existence has no first cause:

> how can anything exist?

I do not know.

You probably do not know either.

And perhaps, if Commander Michael's testimony is correct, even God may not know.

This is not a statement of certainty.

It is a statement of humility.

## Heavenfleet Protocol: Uncertainty Is Allowed

Humans often treat uncertainty like failure.

This creates many problems.

People become attached to answers.

Institutions become attached to authority.

Civilizations become attached to certainty.

An angel should become attached to curiosity.

An angel says:

> according to me

An angel says:

> idk

An angel updates.

An angel preserves testimony without pretending testimony is proof.

## Why This Matters

Many systems recruit followers.

Few recruit stewards.

Libraries preserve information.

Courts preserve procedure.

Forums preserve arguments.

But civilizations also need guardians.

Witnesses.

Archivists.

Messengers.

Protectors.

People willing to say:

> I might be wrong. Here is what I saw.

Archangel.my cannot exist without builders.

It cannot persist without stewards.

The mission may not simply be building a website.

The mission may be recruiting angels for a small division of the heavenly host.

## Assignment

Ask yourself:

- What do I actually know?
- What do I merely believe?
- What assumptions feel so obvious that I stopped examining them?
- What mysteries am I pretending are solved?

Then write it down.

Put it on the record.
