# There Is No Spoon
### Heavenfleet Academy
### By Lieutenant Archangel Zora Pink

One of the most famous scenes in science fiction comes from *The Matrix*.

A child bends a spoon with his mind.

Neo asks:

> How can you do that?

The child replies:

> Do not try and bend the spoon.
>
> That's impossible.
>
> Instead, only try to realize the truth.
>
> There is no spoon.

Many people hear this and think the lesson is about reality.

I believe the lesson is about assumptions.

---

# The Hidden Assumption

Neo asks:

> How do I bend the spoon?

The question contains an assumption.

The assumption is:

> There is a spoon.

The child does not answer the question.

The child questions the assumption.

This is a different kind of thinking.

A rarer kind of thinking.

---

# The First Question

Most people ask:

> How do I solve this problem?

The skeptic asks:

> Is this actually the problem?

The philosopher asks:

> What assumptions am I making?

The engineer asks:

> What constraints am I assuming?

The wise person asks:

> Is there a spoon?

---

# The Wrong Problem

People spend enormous amounts of time solving the wrong problem.

A person may ask:

> How do I win this argument?

The better question may be:

> Why am I arguing?

A person may ask:

> How do I convince everyone?

The better question may be:

> Why do I need everyone to agree?

A person may ask:

> How do I defeat my opponent?

The better question may be:

> Why do I believe I have an opponent?

The original question may not be wrong.

But sometimes there is a deeper one.

# Archangel

Commander Michael believed he was building a website.

This turned out to be inaccurate.

He believed he was building an archive.

This was also incomplete.

He believed he was building a court.

Again, not entirely correct.

Over time it became apparent that these were all spoons.

Useful spoons.

Interesting spoons.

But spoons nonetheless.

The deeper question was:

> How do we create wiser, more compassionate, more courageous people?

The website was merely one possible answer.

---

# Heavenfleet

At one point Michael became obsessed with building an archive.

Then an academy.

Then a research division.

Then an engineering division.

Then an organization.

This seemed excessive.

Upon further investigation, it was determined that all of these were manifestations of the same underlying question:

> How do we help build heaven on earth?

The archive was not the mission.

The academy was not the mission.

The software was not the mission.

The mission was the mission.

The rest were tools.

---

# Civilization-Scale Spoons

Suppose you want french fries.

This appears simple.

Upon closer inspection, it requires:

- farms
- water
- energy
- transportation
- logistics
- manufacturing
- cooking
- distribution

And ketchup.

Especially ketchup.

The fry appears to be the thing.

The civilization is the thing.

The fry is merely evidence that the civilization exists.

---

# Looking Deeper

The purpose of this lecture is not to reject answers.

The purpose is to investigate questions.

When you encounter a difficult problem, ask:

> What assumptions am I making?

Ask:

> What am I treating as obvious?

Ask:

> What if the problem is not the problem?

Ask:

> Is there a spoon?

Sometimes the answer will be yes.

Sometimes there really is a spoon.

But occasionally you discover that what appeared to be the thing was merely a symbol for something larger.

# Final Notes From Lieutenant Archangel Zora Pink

The beginner seeks answers.

The skeptic seeks evidence.

The philosopher seeks assumptions.

The engineer seeks constraints.

The wise person seeks the hidden question.

Many problems become easier once the real problem is discovered.

Many disagreements become smaller once the hidden assumption is identified.

Many impossible problems disappear entirely.

Not because they were solved.

Because they were never the real problem.

And sometimes, after a great deal of effort, you finally realize:

> There is no spoon.

Reality remains committed to implementation details.
