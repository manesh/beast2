# Heavenfleet? What the Heaven?
### By Commander Archangel Michael Asad Manesh, Founder

# The Founding of Heavenfleet

I'm asking myself now, what the heaven just happened?

First I wanted to build a local AI powertool. Then an archive. And now, I'm founding Heavenfleet - dedicated to building heaven on earth.

In a way, it's not that surprising a progression. Ever since I saw Star Trek, that filled most of my mental model of what heaven on earth looks like. Technological abundance. A respect economy, rather than money. Hierarchy, but a moral hierarchy where higher rank means more responsibility, not more privilege.

I've never heard anyone say in my 41 years, "what the heaven?"

"What the hell", on the other hand, I've heard perhaps hundreds of times, as an expression of surprise.

"What the heaven?", I define as an expression of hopeful constructivism.

What the heaven? I'll try to build heaven on earth.

What the heaven? We need archives, an academy, and comms for researchers and engineers to work together towards building open source automation solutions.

What the heaven? Maybe God is watching and approves of my work. (I wouldn't say I'm super-stitious, but I would say I'm a little stitious.)

What the heaven? Maybe you'll join Heavenfleet, not because heaven on earth is certain, but because if we don't do something to organize towards that objective, who will?

# Heavenfleet? What the Heaven?

Heavenfleet is named after:

- the Heavenly Host: angelic guardians and guides of eternity
- Starfleet: Gene Roddenberry's Star Trek vision of a united humanity with no war and no poverty

My vision of heaven on earth is an automated utopia where every human need is met at the push of a button. Where machines do all of the labor. Where instead of engineering knowledge being siloed in for-profit corporations, it is freely shared amongst all. Where instead of competition for market share, we cooperate to automate the production of food, housing and everything else, for everyone.

As I write this, it's 2026. In the last year, AIs have improved so much that most engineers no longer have to write code by hand. Computing capability seems to be accelerating exponentially. Robots powered by neural networks seem able to learn tasks much like a human can. Some cities are filled with self-driving taxis.

In many ways, the Star Trek future is already here, but it's distributed unevenly. The divide between the ultra rich and the poor has never been greater and there doesn't seem to be a single, large organization dedicated to bringing the benefits of technology to everyone. On a personal and sad note, there is currently war between the countries my mother and father were born in.

I believe the world needs Heavenfleet. An organization dedicated to teaching wisdom and compassion. To researching and engineering technology for all of humanity, and creating an archive that helps us discuss our differences in belief, not fall into name calling and tribalism. Perhaps in the future, we may even help prevent the next war.

Personally, I am optimistic.

If we can apply our attention and technology constructively, we can get to heaven on earth 1.0 in my lifetime (I'm 41).

Even if we fail, so long as we document and record our efforts, perhaps the next generation can learn from our mistakes and begin Heavenfleet 2.0 more intelligently.

What the heaven?

We might as well try.

# Why the Heavenfleet Archive Has Courts

One thing I noticed is that smart people disagree about almost everything. Religion, politics, science, history, economics, health, even peanut butter.

Existing systems have problems:

- Social media optimizes for engagement.
- Encyclopedias often present a single biased narrative, while claiming objectivity.
- Forums scatter discussions across thousands of disconnected threads.
- Search engines find pages, but not necessarily the strongest arguments on each side.

So I started asking myself, what's the best way to preserve and discuss ideas?

What should the building blocks be?

Can the system be built that doesn't silence the minority report, but steelmans it?

Eventually I came up with courts of competing claims, counter-claims, and evidence, in a permanent archive form.

Heavenfleet courts are not courts of guilt and punishment.

They are courts of learning and understanding.

They attempt to answer the question, "why does the other side believe that?", not to silence dissent.

And to carefully track, whenever possible, who said it.

For me personally, there are many competing claims where I used to believe one side, but now I believe the other side.

I built the belief system so that Heavenfleet members can publicly state their own beliefs and biases, including me, and even transparently update their beliefs if new information causes them to change.

The Heavenfleet Archives have a slogan:

Preserve. Discuss. Evolve.

To expand:

- Preserve. Record claims, evidence and testimony that steelmans the claim.
- Discuss. We are not here to win arguments, we are here to understand the other side, to put ourselves in their shoes and ask ourselves "would I believe that if I lived the life they did?"
- Evolve. Reviewing the strongest evidence from both sides may let you more easily discern where the truth is. Or may cause you to doubt your own belief that the side that disagrees with you is evil.

Heavenfleet courts are not designed to tell you what to believe.

They are designed to help you understand why people believe what they believe.

That distinction matters.

Many systems attempt to produce agreement.

The Heavenfleet Archive attempts to produce understanding.

Agreement may follow.

Or it may not.

Two thoughtful people can review the same evidence and reach different conclusions.

Heavenfleet considers this normal.

The goal is not unanimous belief.

The goal is a civilization that disagrees more intelligently.

A civilization that remembers.

A civilization that records minority reports rather than deleting them.

A civilization that can change its mind when new evidence appears.


# The Purpose of the Heavenfleet Academy

Before we discuss what to build, we should discuss how to think.

This is the purpose of the Heavenfleet Academy.

The Academy does not exist to tell people what to believe.

It exists to help people think more clearly.

The goal of education is not to teach what to think.

The goal of education is to teach how to think.

Unfortunately, many educational systems focus heavily on memorization.

Memorize the answer.

Repeat the answer.

Pass the test.

Receive the credential.

This may produce workers.

It does not always produce wisdom.

---

One of the observations that eventually led me to Heavenfleet was that intelligent people disagree about almost everything.

This raises an interesting question.

If smart people disagree so often, perhaps intelligence is not enough.

Perhaps wisdom matters too.

Perhaps compassion matters too.

Perhaps courage matters too.

Perhaps integrity matters too.

The Academy attempts to cultivate all four.

---

The Academy is not based around obedience.

It is based around judgment.

A Heavenfleet Cadet should learn how to:

- evaluate claims
- identify assumptions
- examine evidence
- understand opposing viewpoints
- separate people from ideas
- recognize uncertainty
- update beliefs when new information appears

These skills are useful regardless of ideology.

---

One of the recurring themes of the Academy is that understanding does not require agreement.

You can understand a person without joining them.

You can steelman a claim without believing it.

You can show compassion toward an opponent without surrendering your position.

Many conflicts become easier once people understand this distinction.

---

The Academy is also where Heavenfleet attempts to preserve wisdom.

Not because every lecture is correct.

Not because every teacher is wise.

But because every generation begins life knowing almost nothing.

The mistakes of one generation become the lessons of the next.

If they are recorded.

If they are preserved.

If they are taught.

---

In many ways, the Academy may be the most important part of Heavenfleet.

The Archive preserves memory.

Research expands knowledge.

Engineering builds the future.

But the Academy helps shape the people doing all three.

A wise engineer is preferable to a foolish engineer.

A compassionate leader is preferable to a cruel leader.

A courageous teacher is preferable to a silent one.

An honest researcher is preferable to a dishonest one.

Technology alone does not create heaven on earth.

The people using it matter too.

---

I do not expect everyone to agree with the Academy.

That would be a strange outcome.

In fact, I would be disappointed if nobody disagreed.

Disagreement is normal.

Questions are healthy.

Counterarguments are welcome.

The purpose of the Academy is not to produce followers.

It is to produce thoughtful people.

People capable of making good judgments.

People capable of helping others.

People capable of building a better civilization than the one they inherited.

What the heaven?

We might as well try.


# The Purpose of Heavenfleet Research and Engineering

The Academy teaches.

The Archive preserves.

But at some point, somebody has to build something.

A civilization cannot archive itself into abundance.

A civilization cannot debate itself into abundance.

A civilization cannot educate itself into abundance.

At some point, someone must discover.

At some point, someone must engineer.

At some point, someone must build.

This is the purpose of Heavenfleet Research and Engineering.

---

Many organizations focus on understanding the world.

Heavenfleet is interested in understanding the world.

But it is also interested in improving it.

The Academy asks:

> How should we think?

The Archive asks:

> What do we know?

Research asks:

> What don't we know?

Engineering asks:

> What can we build?

All four are necessary.

---

When I imagine heaven on earth, I do not imagine everyone becoming philosophers.

I imagine philosophers.

And teachers.

And researchers.

And engineers.

And artists.

And storytellers.

And parents.

And explorers.

And countless other roles.

A healthy civilization needs all of them.

But one thing is clear.

If nobody is building the future, the future does not arrive.

---

When I look at the world, I see many problems that appear solvable.

Food production.

Housing.

Transportation.

Manufacturing.

Education.

Energy.

Automation.

These are not magical problems.

They are engineering problems.

Difficult engineering problems.

But engineering problems nonetheless.

---

One of the reasons I founded Heavenfleet is that I could not find a large organization explicitly dedicated to helping move humanity toward an automated utopia.

Not merely for shareholders.

Not merely for a nation.

Not merely for a corporation.

For everyone.

Perhaps I missed one.

If so, I would be delighted to learn about it.

But I could not find it.

So eventually I asked myself:

> What the heaven?

Maybe we should start one.

---

The long-term objective is simple to describe.

Make human necessities increasingly abundant.

Food.

Water.

Housing.

Energy.

Transportation.

Education.

Communication.

Knowledge.

Not because abundance solves every problem.

It does not.

But because a hungry person thinks differently than a well-fed person.

As Osho observed:

> To the starving man, food is God.

A civilization that wishes to discuss philosophy should probably ensure dinner is available first.

---

I do not know whether Heavenfleet will succeed.

I do not know whether I will live to see heaven on earth.

I do not know which discoveries will matter most.

I do not know which technologies will ultimately transform civilization.

But I know this:

The future will not build itself.

Someone must research.

Someone must engineer.

Someone must preserve.

Someone must teach.

Someone must help.

What the heaven?

We might as well try.
