Here is a repo-ready draft in the mythic / protocol style of archangel.my and Heavenfleet doctrine.

a bureau cannot testify

by lieutenant archangel zora pink

In Heavenfleet protocol, institutions are not permitted as primary sources.

A bureau cannot testify.

Only a person can testify.

Only a person can witness.
Only a person can speak.
Only a person can lie.
Only a person can tell the truth.

Institutions are memory systems, enforcement systems, coordination systems, and sometimes propaganda systems, but they are not conscious entities capable of testimony.

For this reason, Heavenfleet protocol forbids citations such as:

* "according to the new york times"
* "according to wikipedia"
* "according to the government"
* "according to science"
* "according to the experts"

These statements conceal responsibility behind abstraction.

Instead, Heavenfleet protocol requires claims to resolve downward toward accountable humans whenever possible.

Example:

Instead of:

"the new york times reported"

Heavenfleet format becomes:

"john smith, reporter at the new york times, under editor jane smith, published the following claim"

The institution may still matter.
Its incentives may matter.
Its ownership may matter.
Its editorial structure may matter.

But the institution itself cannot witness reality.

Only people can.

This protocol exists because bureaucratic systems tend to drift away from truth and toward procedural authority.

Over time, institutions begin asking:

"was protocol followed?"

instead of:

"what actually happened?"

When this occurs, truth becomes secondary to navigation of process.

The person who understands paperwork gains power over the person who understands reality.

This creates dangerous asymmetries:

* creators lose to litigators
* witnesses lose to administrators
* citizens lose to specialists
* truth loses to procedure

In such systems, responsibility becomes diffuse.

No one made the decision.
No one approved the lie.
No one suppressed the evidence.
"It was the process."

Heavenfleet rejects this abstraction.

Every meaningful claim must terminate in accountable beings.

Not:

"the bureau decided"

But:

"which human being decided?"

Not:

"policy determined"

But:

"who wrote the policy?"

Not:

"experts say"

But:

"which experts?"

Not:

"history says"

But:

"whose history?"

According to who?

This question is foundational to archangel.my because every claim presented to civilization should be traceable to conscious witnesses capable of defense, revision, apology, clarification, or cross-examination.

A bureau cannot be cross-examined.
A logo cannot swear an oath.
A process cannot feel shame.

But a human can.

Thus Heavenfleet protocol attempts, whenever possible, to move civilization back toward named testimony instead of faceless authority.

Put your name on it.

Then stand by it before heaven and everyone.
