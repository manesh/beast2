Scenario 1: The Rageposter

Cadet Steve arrives.

Immediately posts:

THIS COURT IS CORRUPT.

EVERYONE HERE IS AN IDIOT.

YOU’RE ALL BRAINWASHED.

Thirty-seven replies appear.

The first thirty-five make things worse.

The thirty-sixth says:

Cadet, I think you should read "Why Smart People Believe Different Things."

Steve replies:

NO.

Three days later Steve returns.

Posts:

Fine. I read it.

I still think you’re wrong.

Progress detected.

⸻

Two months later:

Here is my actual argument.

A miracle has occurred.

Scenario 2: The Conspiracy Cadet

Cadet Sarah arrives.

Posts forty-seven claims in a single evening.

Every one of them is:

THEY LIED TO US.

Someone replies:

According to who?

Sarah becomes angry.

⸻

Three weeks later:

Okay.

According to John Smith.

According to Mary Jones.

According to my own observations.

The lecture worked.

Not because it changed her conclusions.

Because it improved her sourcing.

Scenario 3: The Debunker

Cadet James arrives.

Believes his mission is to save everyone from misinformation.

Every reply is:

Debunked.

Every discussion becomes:

Here is why you’re wrong.

Someone eventually posts:

I think you need to read "Steelman Before You Counter."

James disappears.

⸻

Returns six months later.

Posts:

Before I explain why I disagree, here’s the strongest version of your position as I understand it.

Entire court stunned.

Long-Term Projection

Commander,

I suspect the Academy will not work the way you imagine.

Most people will not read the lectures.

Many will read only one.

Some will hate them.

Some will misunderstand them.

Some will quote them incorrectly.

A few will internalize them.

⸻

The surprising thing is that you do not need everyone.

You only need enough.

Enough people who begin responding to:

You’re an idiot.

with:

According to who?

Enough people who begin responding to:

Everything is hopeless.

with:

What the heaven?

Enough people who begin responding to:

Why doesn’t somebody fix this?

with:

Maybe I’m somebody.

⸻

If that happens often enough for long enough, culture emerges.

And culture is the thing you’re actually building.

The archive is just where you store it.
