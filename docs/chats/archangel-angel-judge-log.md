Judge

A judge is not a moderator.

A judge is any person who stands before the record and says:

believe
idk
reject

Maybe later:

suspect
doubt

But if you want the cleanest court-of-heaven framing, the basic judicial act is:

believe / idk / reject

A judge may offer:

judgment
reason
evidence cited
date
change history

Example:

Judge Michael says:
believe
Reason:
the testimony is consistent with the documents
Date:
May 21, 2026

Or:

Judge Sarah says:
reject
Reason:
the claim confuses temporal sequence with causation

The public record is then:

Judged by everyone:
42 believe
19 idk
31 reject

This makes "judge" a universal role.

Every user is a judge because every user may judge.

That is strong.

⸻

Angel

An angel is above a judge because an angel has powers.

An angel is not merely someone with an opinion.
An angel can help shape the visible record.

An angel may be able to:

add evidence
add counterevidence
add testimony
add claims
organize sections
mark duplicates
challenge weak cards
suggest summaries
restore buried minority reports
help preserve the record

Depending on how much power you want to grant, angels can have levels of power without adding more ranks.

Example:

angel powers:
- add cards
- edit summaries
- organize sections
- collapse spam
- propose removals
- verify testimony packets

But the rank remains one word:

angel

The important rule:

Angels are appointed by the archangel.

Or more mythic:

An angel receives wings from the archangel.

⸻

Archangel

Archangel is your self-given rank.

That works if the site is honest about it.

The UI should not pretend this is neutral institutional authority. It should say plainly:

archangel:
Michael, founder of archangel.my
This is my heaven.
The record is public.
The judgments are public.
The powers are granted by me.
The decisions are judged by everyone.

That is coherent.

It is personal, mythic, and transparent.

The danger would be pretending it is decentralized when it is not.
The strength is saying:

this is Michael’s court of heaven, and everyone can judge the court.

That makes the sovereignty explicit.

archangel.my has three public ranks:
- judge
- angel
- archangel
## Judge
A judge is anyone who gives a public judgment on a claim.
A judgment is not truth.

A judge may say:
- believe
- idk
- reject
Each judgment may include a reason, cited evidence, and a date.
Judgments may change.  
The history of changed judgments is preserved.
archangel.my is built by Michael and judged by everyone.

Angels help preserve, organize, and improve the record.
Angels may add evidence, add counterevidence, organize claim pages, challenge weak items, mark duplicates, and help maintain the court.
Angel powers are not a claim of truth.  
Angel powers are a grant of trust.
The archangel decides who receives angel powers.
## Archangel
The archangel is Michael, founder of archangel.my.
The archangel grants angel powers, guards the structure of the court, and remains publicly judged by the record.
The archangel does not make truth true.
The archangel maintains this heaven.

Everyone may judge.

The archangel is Michael.
I built this court.
I grant angel powers.
I guard this heaven as best I can.
But I am not God.
My judgments are judgments.
My decisions are part of the record.
And the record is judged by everyone.
