the night i chose the name heavenfleet over archangel
log june 9 2026
commander michael asad manesh, archangel of heavenfleet

ive been calling it heavenfleet for several weeks now, i think. but it occurred to me that should be the name rather than archangel.my only a few hours ago, after i had my ai run a search and they found zero results.

archangel? some government archive project to blockchain document provenance. not the same as my testimony-first, approach, but still

plus i realized "i founded heavenfleet" will be more searchable than "i founded archangel".

but the real reason is building culture. ive written or had my ai write some 20 lectures so far. ive been thinking about how to build not just an archive, but recruit angels who want to build heaven on earth. an archive is just one piece.

heavenfleet: join and help build heaven on earth, an automated utopia where every human need is met at the push of a button. where machines do most of the work. where there is abundance of food and housing, where disagreements lead to discussion, research and sharing, not conflict.

Jesus said "man does not survive on bread alone."

Osho, my favorite spiritual teacher, said, "to the starving man, food is god."

perhaps one needs a full belly before they can easily contemplate philosophy, spirtuality and morality. which is why one of heavenfleet's missions is to design, open source and build fully automated food production for everyone.

one thing i believe is names have power. heavenfleet communicates that this is a hierarchical group with a mission. i may be the first commander of heavenfleet, but i wont be the last, i intend to train replacements for me. my goal is to train as many souls into wise, compassionate, courageous behavior as i can.

ideally, everyone.

many people ask "are you thinking small enough and shipping an mvp (minimum viable product?)" i like to ask the counter claim: am i being ambitious enough? am i not thinking big picture and long term enough? am i aiming at heaven, or just a slightly less worse hell?

i wanted to be starfleet long before i wanted to be an angel. the post-scarcity, computerized utopia of star trek was the first vision of a heaven on earth i ever saw. it still wasnt perfect, but it was grand and nearly so, and kept improving. star trek inspired me to become a programmer/engineer and keep up with the latest tech, contributing as i could.

but the last few years, i started asking hard questions. why doesnt the government have a department of automated food production? why dont people get along? is me just taking jobs and letting others lead getting us to a techno utopia, almost a heaven?

my purpose in life hasnt really changed that much since i was a kid. build a techno-heaven. now i see a way i can meaningfully contribute. to build something that hasnt been built before. not just an open source project, or a forum, but an orgnization with an academy and public archives, preserving as much condensed wisdom as possible, and bringing people together in a constructive way.

so ill keep building. what was archangel.my now becomes the heavenfleet archive and the heavenfleet academy.

hopefully soon ill launch and start recruiting angels. maybe youre one of them.

lets build heaven on earth together, an automated utopia where every human need is met at the push of a button.

or i guess voice command.

as captain Picard would say, computer, earl grey tea, hot.
