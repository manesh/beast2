I think your underlying objection is stronger than the exact wording you proposed banning.

Your real objection seems to be:

institutions and experts often smuggle authority claims into passive language.

often actually means:

"no evidence accepted by the institutions I trust."

That is a fair epistemic criticism.

Your "according to who?" framework usefully forces:

* named observers,
* named institutions,
* explicit standards,
* explicit scope,
* and acknowledgment of uncertainty.

That is valuable.

But I would strongly caution against banning phrases merely because they can imply overreach.

Because:

"no reproducible evidence yet accepted under rigorous measurement standards"

does not logically require omniscience.

A person can reasonably mean:

"I am unaware of any replication accepted by the relevant scientific communities under methodologies they consider rigorous."

That is a bounded statement, not a god claim.

The problem is not skepticism itself.
The problem is hidden scope and hidden authority.

So instead of banning the phrase entirely, archangel.my might become stronger by forcing specificity.

For example, require claims like:

Instead of:

"there is no evidence"

Require:

"I am unaware of independently replicated demonstrations accepted by X engineering institutions under Y testing standards as of DATE."

That is much more precise and honest.

Likewise, proponents should also be constrained symmetrically.

Instead of:

"physics is wrong"

Require:

"I believe current mainstream electromagnetic models incompletely explain these observations because…"

That preserves debate while reducing rhetorical inflation.

One danger I see:
if archangel.my becomes hostile toward mainstream standards entirely, you risk building a system where:

* any unsupported claim survives indefinitely,
* criticism becomes taboo,
* and rigor collapses into identity politics.

But your core insight remains useful:

authority claims should be attributable.

That principle cuts both ways.

A scientist should not be able to hide behind:

"science says."

But neither should an inventor be able to hide behind:

"they suppressed me."

Both sides should present:

* names,
* measurements,
* methods,

Ironically, your best version of archangel.my may not destroy scientific rigor -- it may force scientific rhetoric to become more explicit and personally accountable.
