added markdown-like syntax, claims can be wikipedia-like articles with links to other claims, public figures, and evidence

example opposing claims where 2 public figures disagree





Example:

# claim: lab leak is a plausible origin of covid-19
summary:
The origin of covid-19 is unresolved. One public claim is that a laboratory-associated incident remains a plausible explanation.
supports:
- [public figure: Jon Stewart] argued that the Wuhan lab proximity made the lab-leak theory worth taking seriously.
- [public figure: Senator Rand Paul] has argued that gain-of-function research and lab safety questions should remain under investigation.
opposes:
- [public figure: Dr. Anthony Fauci] argued that available scientific evidence favored natural spillover, while acknowledging investigation should continue.
- [public figure: Peter Daszak] argued that wildlife-origin explanations remained more likely than a lab accident.
evidence:
- [evidence: US intelligence assessments on covid origins]
- [evidence: WHO covid origins reports]
- [evidence: early Wuhan market case clustering studies]
related claims:
- [[covid-19 originated through natural spillover]]
- [[gain-of-function research increases pandemic risk]]
- [[intelligence agencies should publicly disclose covid-origin evidence]]
status:
contested

Core idea: a claim page is not just a statement. It is a mini courtroom/wikipedia node: who says it, who disputes it, what evidence is cited, and what adjacent claims it depends on.
