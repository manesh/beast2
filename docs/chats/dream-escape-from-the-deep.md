This is a story. A story about a dream.

This is the truth. The truth of what I remember: my time as a slave.

Passing by in the tunnel.

There was one tunnel that connected the deep and the shallow. Every time I had to go through it, if someone else was there, I had to walk all the way back and start again, because I was the lowest.

The lowest.

I was the weakest. Not physically, for I was big, but mentally. I wasn’t strong. I couldn’t fight, not even when I was clearly right. Not even though I carried a sword could I fight. Not even though I had a weapon could I kill. I always submitted because I was the weakest. I was the smallest-hearted.

So I thought.

I’m writing this now because I might forget, and I don’t want to forget, even though it’s the worst thing that ever happened to me.

I was taken.

And it doesn’t matter from where or when. I could say this: it wasn’t that bad. It was some kind of life that I thought was good, although seeing the future--the future I hope for--the life was terrible. New line. But I’m not there yet. I’m still here, just out of the worst experience of my life, having a piece of paper for the first time, and a recording device. But what’s the difference? Consistent storage.

In the passing by, if someone else was there, I had to move out of the way. I had to walk all the way back to the start of the tunnel because two could not pass, and this was by design. It was a security feature. We were never supposed to pass. So if I started moving away, they could stop me. I was like a janitor. I don’t know what I was. No, I was an assistant. No, I was a slave.

There was another slave, a woman, and a lot I don’t remember the details of. New line. Or I don’t want to.

But she was there at the end of the pathway, across the corridor. She had made a secret, and I found out, and I finished it, and that’s how we escaped. We flew out of there together, and this was why I’m able to write this.

I got out of there. We got out of there. We escaped. She did most of the work. I just completed the device.

I hated that corridor. I hated squeezing past. Sometimes, if the slave was just as low as me, we’d squeeze past. She was one of these slaves, I think. There were so many that were just as low as me. There were only a few I really had to watch out for. But this culture was so strange. Why did I have a weapon? I had a weapon.

Maybe we just ran away, but that’s not what I remember. I remember killing, but I don’t remember who I killed. He was somehow the leader. He was somehow the boss. He somehow was confronting me when I was trying to escape with her. So I took my weapon, and I stabbed him in the heart. And I stabbed, and I stabbed, and I stabbed.

And everyone who thought I was the lowest, who thought I never would kill, who thought my sword was a joke, was shocked. So shocked they just let me pass.

Because I was the lowest, but I killed the highest.

I was the lowest, but I killed the highest.

Because I finally found a way out.

And I finally found someone to escape with.

And that’s what gave me the power. That’s what gave me the heart. That’s what gave me the strength. And that’s why I’m writing this.

I escaped.

I escaped. I killed.

This is what I remember from my dream.








There is more to my dream.

I think she was keeping the last piece a secret, but the details don’t matter. I could just make them up for a storybook, but that’s not what I’m trying to do here. I’m just trying to get the dream down.

This was a place of true culture, not the fake culture I had lived in before I was stolen and taken to the deep.

I think maybe I was born a slave.
