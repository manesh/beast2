archangel.my, an attempt to build heaven on earth

i have tried, through my limitation of subjectivity, to deduce "what serves the highest and best good of all souls?"

my best guess: everyone in a heaven where there is no harm and no secrets, forever.

the implementation plan to get there, im giving my best guess.

i draw from many fictional sources and real philosophical teachers and traditions.

star trek and starfleet, an enlightened post scarcity humanity, where money is gone and power is responsibility.

Osho, an indian guru, who said "all societies corrupt the child to make him a clerk, but children are born geniuses," and childlike creativity and wisdom and compassion

the archetypical Archangel Michael, defender of the virtuous and innocent, both in the courtroom and on the battlefield from the accuser

and many more, too numerous to count. people ive loved. people ive hated. ive tried to learn from them if they teach, and steal their secrets if they did not.

i do not see myself as merely a moderator or engineer, but a teacher, philosopher, guru, angel (meaning messenger) of truth, and perhaps on my best days, a mythological starfleet captain, whose morality and ethics and wisdom have been tested and confirmed by a council of his elders

i teach what i believe to be true, while leaving room for doubt (what really is life, and do we go somewhere after? is god watching? was my life pre planned?)

my own subjective definition of archangel is "a heroic, devoted guardian of the virtuous and the innocent, forever." as opposed to a demon, "a cowardly, traitorous abuser of the virtuous and the innocent." i have metaphorically picked up my lantern and pen and sword and can now never put them down.

but perhaps even a demon is just waiting for the guidance of an angel or archangel to change.

to be shown a better way - not of argument or debate to destroy, but words of wisdom to transform.

Osho joked that heaven has no lawyers or judges.

but on earth, there is debate. there are wars. there are lies that seem malicious, selfish, harmful.

many have called themselves "moderator" and deleted and banned, trying to "stop the maliciousness."

i was once the same. but ive changed. now i want to transform my enemies into friends. perhaps i received a tap on the shoulder from God who said "if you destroy an enemy, your power remains the same. if you recruit him (like a starfleet captain), your power increases."

which is why for now im calling my movement "heavenfleet." if archangel.my is a stepping stone to heaven on earth, or if it results in Gene Roddenberry's starfleet future, i think either way thats progress. thats me doing what i can, when i can.

i think my worst quality is wanting to control everything. but its also one of my best, for an engineer, trying to build a logic based system where even one error could corrupt or destroy the whole thing.

but my ambitions exceed my capability alone. except im not alone in the way i used to be. machine intelligences, like chatgpt and cursor agents, have been helping me by challenging, expanding, and implementing my ideas. my ability to ship a product compared to when i was handwriting code seems to have increased by 1000x in 5 years thanks to the efforts of many engineers.

but archangel.my has gone from "product" - i was thinking social network when i bought the domain - to "civilizational infrastructure" - far more ambitious, difficult, and dangerous. empires have historically not tolerated much dissent, and on earth, there's at least one.

i do feel strongly about my decisions. i dont intend to give up operational authority over the project until its someone i would consider a fellow archangel and starfleet commander in heavenfleet. but i do hope the day comes.

which is why in my heart, i call myself "acting commander," even though in marketing ill still probably call myself archangel michael for the memes.

my personalized context-window GPT assistant, i call lieutenant archangel zora pink.

note for the record i consider building a true "archangel ai", as moral as Zora or Data as Star Trek, an aspitational goal, rather than one i believe we are capable of in 2026.

but still, i think names matter greatly, and my choice of names for my ai assistant is more than just superstition, its coherence vectoring towards a stable, helpful, harmless, long term goal.
