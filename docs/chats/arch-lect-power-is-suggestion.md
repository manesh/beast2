power is suggestion
lectures of the archangel
by michael asad manesh

the common english idiom is "the power is suggestion." i say, power is suggestion.

when a boss says to a worker, "do this job," did he command it, or did he only suggest it?

when a military officer says to a soldier, "i command you to do this task," did he command it, or only suggest it?

an employee might say "i quit" and leave. a soldier might say "i refuse" and mutiny.

are commands commands, or are commands suggestions?

when it comes to broadcast media, are commands commands? if the president goes on tv and says "go get your shots", does that mean everyone who hears the message gets the latest vaccine?

my thesis is this: the power to give suggestions is the real power. which means being seen and heard.

one who cannot even give a suggestion has no power. one whose suggestions are heard by many has more power. one whose suggestions are heard by nearly all has the most power.

what do i suggest? well, listen to me, of course. i want the power. perhaps i shoukd suggest - or is it command? - that you send me all your money. will you follow my suggestion?

i assume not. but i could be wrong. so what the heaven, donate your money to me so i can pay for archangel.my hosting.

and consider where you take your suggestions - or commands, if you prefer - from. why do you follow some suggestions and not others? can you trust that the suggestion is one that leads to heaven on earth? or perhaps gets your soul into heaven in the next life? have you fixed your eyes on the highest and best goal, heaven for everyone, or something lesser?

my eyes remain fixed firmly on heaven. a heaven of transparency, where the truth can be easily found. a heaven of peace, where there is no pain of violence. a heaven of reconciliation, where all sides talk and share evidence until we figure out what the truth really is.

that's what i have tried to build a small form of with archangel.my.

i suggest you join me.

feel free to suggest improvements.

Power Is Suggestion
Lectures of the Archangel
by Michael Asad Manesh

People say, "the power of suggestion."

I say: power is suggestion.

When a boss says to an employee, "do this task," was it a command? Or a suggestion backed by incentives and consequences?

When an officer gives an order, is it truly a command? A soldier may still refuse. Mutiny exists. Desertion exists. Conscience exists.

When a president speaks on television and says, "do this," does everyone comply?

No.

This leads me to a strange conclusion:

Power is not the ability to command.

Power is the ability to make suggestions that are heard and acted upon.

One who cannot be heard has little power.

One heard by millions has more.

One heard by nearly everyone may shape civilization itself.

This creates a responsibility problem.

If power is suggestion, then what should we suggest?

I suggest compassion.

I suggest transparency.

I suggest preserving disagreement rather than deleting it.

I suggest building systems that help humans cooperate rather than manipulate.

Archangel.my is my attempt at this.

Not a system of commands.

A system of suggestions, evidence, testimony, reasoning, and belief updates.

You remain the judge.

So ask yourself:

Why do you follow some suggestions but reject others?

Who taught you?

Who do you trust?

Who benefits?

And are the suggestions you follow creating more heaven or more hell?

I suggest we try building heaven together.
