This is a story about a dream.

I was a slave in the deep.

There was a tunnel between the deep and the shallow, too narrow for two people to pass. Whenever someone came the other way, I had to turn back and start again, because I was the lowest.

I carried a sword, but everyone knew I would never use it. I was big, but weak in heart. I could not fight, even when I was right. I could not kill, even with a weapon in my hand.

So I thought.

There was another slave, a woman. She came to the deep only sometimes, and when she did, she worked in secret on a craft shaped like an orb, with seats and controls on top. She built most of it. I only found the last piece and finished it.

Together, we escaped.

When the highest one tried to stop us, I drew my sword and stabbed him in the heart. I stabbed and stabbed until everyone who had laughed at me understood.

I was the lowest, but I killed the highest.

Not because I had become cruel.

Because I had found a way out.

Because I had found someone to escape with.

We flew away in the orb and came to the most beautiful city I had ever seen. Stone fountains shaped like bears, lions, and dolphins poured water forever. Trees, vines, flowers, and gardens filled the streets. It was true culture, not the fake culture of the place I had known before the deep.

Then I wondered if I had always been a slave.

Maybe the place before the deep was only a softer cage. Maybe they had watched me from birth and sent me deeper when they learned I could be used.

I do not know who knew. I do not know who allowed it.

I only know this:

I was taken.

I was used.

I escaped.

And I remember.
