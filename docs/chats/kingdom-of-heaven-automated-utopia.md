Michael’s Kingdom of Heaven on Earth

Michael’s Kingdom of Heaven on Earth is the long-range north star: an automated utopia where every human need can be met at the push of a button.

It is not only an engineering project. It is a political, psychological, and engineering movement: engineering to build the machines and systems, psychology to heal and strengthen the humans who use them, and politics to arrange society so abundance is shared rather than captured.

There may be a king, but the true aim is not a kingdom of subjects. The aim is a kingdom of kings: a world where people are strong, sovereign, generous, and capable. As Osho might say, a world of beggars can only beg from each other, but a world of emperors can give to each other.

This idea has guided Michael’s movement between engineering, psychology, and politics: always asking, how do we get there from here? The answer is to do the neglected work, the work others are not doing, the work that compounds toward heaven on earth instead of merely duplicating effort.

Michael’s personal task is to become the best king he can be: not by domination, but by modeling himself after heroic guardians of the virtuous and innocent -- protectors, builders, truth-keepers, and servants of the future.
